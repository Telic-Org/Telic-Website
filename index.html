Why Telic

Built between rigid bots and AI that rethinks every step.

Most operational workflows are neither perfectly predictable nor fully open-ended. Telic is designed for work with a stable core and a smaller number of moments that need interpretation, adaptation, or review.

01 · MANUAL WORK

Flexible, but difficult to scale

People handle variation well, but volume brings more hiring, training, supervision, and cost.

Best for highly judgment-based work
02 · TRADITIONAL AUTOMATION

Efficient until the path changes

Fixed automation cannot reason through unfamiliar screens or unstructured information, so exceptions and updates create maintenance work.

Best for completely fixed processes
03 · GENERAL AI AGENTS

Capable, but costly at volume

Paying for AI to reason through every routine action makes repeated, high-volume execution unnecessarily expensive.

Best for one-off, exploratory tasks
04 · TELIC

Efficient on the routine. Intelligent on the variable.

Telic reuses proven paths and concentrates AI effort on the parts that genuinely need it.

Best for mostly repeatable workflows with real-world variation

How Telic works

One defined process. Different kinds of execution.

Telic separates routine execution from moments that need intelligence, then verifies the result before the workflow moves on.

Explore the full process →
01

Describe the workflow in natural language

Create a plain-language process definition document (PDD) much like you would explain the work to a person. Telic turns the steps, rules, exceptions, and expected results into a structured workflow.

02

Run familiar steps efficiently

Known and previously verified paths are reused instead of asking an AI model to rediscover every action.

03

Use intelligence where it adds value

AI handles changing information, ambiguity, and recoverable exceptions within the relevant part of the workflow.

04

Verify, record, and continue

Telic checks meaningful outcomes, records what happened, and asks for human guidance when safe continuation is uncertain.
